about me
I am a robot software engineer at Aldebaran Robotics. I design and build immersive behavior and functionality into autonomous humanoid robots. I work on everything from sensor extraction to consumer apps, NLP to front-end web and much in-between.

Prior to that, I spent my time in academia as a researcher in both Human-Robot Interaction, at Tufts University, and Human-Computer Interaction, at Bowling Green State University.

Outside of work, I try to carve out some time for personal projects. I am a big fan of Linux, Emacs, Lisp and functional programming.
